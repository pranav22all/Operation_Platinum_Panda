Properties

Here's a glimpse into a few of the Cheng Foundation's many properties.

The Capitol Building

The Cheng Family owns a number of government properties in and around Washington D.C. Most prominent is their recent purchase of the Capitol Building, the seat of the United States Congress. In addition to owning governmental landmarks, the Cheng Foundation also exerts an unprecedented influence on US politics, with over 500 Congressmen being active members of the Cheng Foundation.

Cheng Family Resorts

A mystic resort located just outside Hong Kong that has been passed down for generations within the Cheng dynasty. Following the death of Bryce and Darrell's uncle, Deng Xiaoping, the Foundation inherited ownership of the resort. Visitors must pay $5 million in order to enter the compound. Certain Cheng family clients such as Xi Jinping are offered a discounted rate of $4,999,999.99.

The Temple of the Twin Dragons

The Temple of the Twin Dragons is where Bryce and Darrell spend the majority of their time. Once a temple, the estates have now been renovated into a lavish palace employing 2000 servants and featuring 17 indoor Jacuzzis and 53 tennis courts with nets hand-crafted out of silver. The palace is most famous for its system of gold-plated toilets which have been emulated by billionaires worldwide.

The Biltmore Estate

The recent purchase of the Biltmore Estate by the Cheng Foundation represents its commitment to preserving American historical treasures. After running the Vanderbilt family bankrupt, the Cheng family generously bought out their estate making it accessible to tourists for the first time in decades.

The Tower of the Twin Dragons

The Tower of the Twin Dragons was an architectural miracle created through Foundation efforts. Beautifully carved into the natural landscape of rural Oregon, it represents the Cheng Foundation's committment to both technological progress and nature. It has since been made a protected National Landmark.
